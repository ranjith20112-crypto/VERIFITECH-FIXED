We verify all types of machine-readable passports (MRPs) and electronic passports (e-passports) following ICAO 9303 standards: (1) Regular/Tourist Passports - Standard travel documents for citizens, (2) Official Passports - For government employees on official duty, (3) Diplomatic Passports - For diplomats and high-ranking officials, (4) Emergency/Temporary Passports - Limited validity travel documents, (5) e-Passports/Biometric Passports - Embedded with electronic chips containing biometric data. Our system supports passports from 190+ countries including all ICAO member states. We can verify both current valid passports and recently expired documents (within 6 months) for identity continuity checks. The system automatically detects document type and applies appropriate validation rules.

MRZ validation is a multi-step process ensuring document authenticity: (1) Format Verification - Checks if the MRZ follows ICAO 9303 format with correct number of lines and characters (TD3 format: 2 lines Ã— 44 characters), (2) Checksum Validation - Validates check digits for document number, date of birth, expiry date, and optional data using modulo 10 algorithm, (3) Data Consistency - Cross-verifies that visual zone information matches MRZ encoded data including name, nationality, and document number, (4) Country Code Validation - Verifies issuing country codes against ISO 3166-1 alpha-3 standards, (5) Date Logic - Checks that dates are valid and expiry is not before issue date. Our OCR engine reads MRZ with 99.8% accuracy even with worn documents, and flags any discrepancies between MRZ data and visual inspection for manual review.

Yes, our forensic analysis detects various types of passport fraud: (1) Photo Substitution - AI compares passport photo with security features and detects signs of photo replacement or manipulation, (2) Data Alteration - Identifies tampered MRZ zones, modified dates, or corrected personal information through font analysis and ink detection, (3) Counterfeit Documents - Validates security features including holograms, watermarks, UV-reactive elements, and microprinting against country-specific standards, (4) Template Fraud - Detects documents created from stolen blank passports or forged using templates, (5) Chip Cloning - For e-passports, verifies digital signatures and chip authenticity to detect cloned or copied chips, (6) Binding Anomalies - Checks for irregular stitching, page replacement, or re-binding signs. Our system flags documents for expert review when confidence scores are below threshold or anomalies are detected.

E-passport verification involves reading and validating the electronic chip embedded in modern biometric passports: (1) NFC Authentication - Uses Basic Access Control (BAC) or Password Authenticated Connection Establishment (PACE) to establish secure communication with the chip, (2) Data Extraction - Reads biometric data (facial image, fingerprints where available), MRZ information, and digital signatures stored in the chip, (3) Digital Signature Verification - Validates the Document Signer Certificate (DSC) and Country Signing Certificate Authority (CSCA) chain to ensure data integrity, (4) Active Authentication - Verifies the chip is genuine and not cloned by challenging the chip to sign data with its private key, (5) Biometric Comparison - Compares the facial image from the chip with a live selfie or existing database photos. This process requires NFC-enabled devices for physical e-passports, but we also support verification through government APIs for chip data validation without physical possession.

Yes, we conduct comprehensive screening against international security databases: (1) Interpol SLTD (Stolen and Lost Travel Documents) - Real-time check against 100+ million reported lost, stolen, and revoked passports from 175+ countries, (2) National Lost Passport Registries - Integration with country-specific databases where available through official channels, (3) Watchlist Screening - Cross-reference against sanctions lists, PEP (Politically Exposed Persons) databases, and adverse media, (4) Visa Overstay Records - Where available through immigration data partnerships, check for previous violations, (5) Cancelled/Revoked Status - Verification if the passport has been invalidated by the issuing authority. Alerts are generated immediately if a passport is flagged in any database, with detailed information about the nature of the alert and recommended actions. This screening is essential for border control, aviation security, and high-risk transaction monitoring.

Our passport verification solution is fully compliant with global data protection standards: (1) GDPR Compliance - We process passport data as a data processor under Article 28, with Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) in place, legal basis documentation, and strict purpose limitation (verification only), (2) Data Minimization - We extract only necessary data points and offer configurable data retention policies with automatic purging, (3) Encryption - All data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256), with secure key management, (4) Consent Management - Built-in workflows for explicit user consent capture and withdrawal mechanisms, (5) ISO 27001 Certified - Our infrastructure and processes meet international information security standards, (6) Local Data Residency - Options for data storage within specific geographic boundaries (India, EU, UAE) to meet local regulations, (7) Audit Trails - Complete logs of all verification activities for compliance reporting and data subject access requests. We never store biometric templates or passport images beyond the contracted retention period.
