For defunct institutions, we verify through multiple channels: (1) University Grants Commission (UGC) records and AICTE archives for approved institutions, (2) Successor universities that absorbed the original institution's records, (3) National Academic Depository (NAD) which maintains permanent records regardless of university status, (4) Physical verification of archived records at university premises, and (5) Affiliation letters from the board/university that granted the original affiliation. We also check historical accreditation status to confirm the institution was recognized during the candidate's study period.

Yes, we verify all modes of education including distance learning, online degrees, and part-time courses. For distance education, we check UGC-DEB (Distance Education Bureau) approval status of the program during the relevant academic year. We verify online degrees through the university's distance education portal and cross-check with the examination division. For foreign online degrees, we verify the institution's accreditation status in its home country and check if the online program was recognized by the relevant education ministry. We also validate examination center records and assignment submissions where applicable.

For provisional certificates, we verify the issued date and confirm if the final degree has been awarded by checking university convocation records. If a candidate is currently pursuing a degree, we can verify enrollment status, expected completion date, and academic standing (if authorized by the candidate) without revealing the job search to the institution. We also check backlog/pending subjects that might delay graduation. For "pursuing" claims, we require enrollment letters and fee receipts as supporting documents, verified directly with the registrar's office.

For foreign education, we verify directly with the issuing institution's registrar office through their official verification channels. For WES (World Education Services) equivalency, we verify the WES reference number and authentication status in the WES database. We also check ICAS (International Credential Assessment Service) and IQAS (International Qualifications Assessment Service) reports for Canadian equivalencies. Our network includes partnerships with verification agencies in the US, UK, Australia, Canada, and Middle East to expedite responses. We handle apostille and embassy attestation verification for documents legalized for use in India.

We employ a multi-layered fraud detection system: (1) Institution legitimacy check against UGC/AICTE/NAAC databases to identify unaccredited "universities", (2) Document forensics analysis including watermark verification, hologram authentication, and paper quality assessment, (3) Font and layout analysis to detect template-based forgeries, (4) Enrollment number pattern validation against university formats, (5) QR code and barcode verification on modern certificates, (6) Cross-referencing with NAD (National Academic Depository) for digitized records, and (7) Physical inspection of security features like seals, stamps, and signatures. We maintain a database of known diploma mills and fake institutions to flag suspicious credentials immediately.
