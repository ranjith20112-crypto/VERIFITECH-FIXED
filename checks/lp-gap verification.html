How Gap Verification Works

Systematic analysis of timeline discontinuities with evidence-based validation

1

Timeline Analysis

Automated scanning of employment and education dates to identify gaps exceeding configurable thresholds (typically 3+ months).

2

Reason Collection

Candidate provides explanation with supporting documents: medical certificates, offer letters, freelance invoices, or travel records.

3

Document Verification

Authentication of supporting evidence through direct contact with hospitals, educational institutions, or previous employers.

4

Reference Cross-check

Telephonic verification with provided references to confirm stated reasons and activities during the gap period.

5

Risk Categorization

Final assessment categorizing gaps as "Explained & Verified," "Partially Explained," or "Unexplained" with risk ratings.

FAQs â€” Gap Verification

A gap is typically defined as any period of 3 months or more (for employment) or 6 months or more (for education) where the candidate cannot account for their activities through documented employment, education, or other verifiable pursuits. However, thresholds are customizable based on industry requirementsâ€”BFSI sectors often scrutinize gaps of 2+ months, while IT sectors may allow 3-6 months for skill upgradation. We flag both "inter-employment gaps" (between jobs) and "intra-employment gaps" (unexplained leaves during employment). The critical factor is not just the duration but the lack of documentation or explanation for the time period.

Medical and maternity gaps are handled with enhanced privacy protection. For medical reasons, we request discharge summaries or fitness certificates from registered hospitals, which we verify directly with the hospital's medical records department without accessing specific diagnosis details (to protect privacy under HIPAA/health data laws). For maternity breaks, we accept birth certificates of children or pediatrician records. We verify the existence of the medical event and duration without necessarily requiring disclosure of specific health conditions unless it affects job capability (e.g., physical roles post-surgery). All medical documentation is handled by specialized teams trained in PHI (Protected Health Information) protocols.

Yes, freelance and self-employment periods are verifiable through multiple channels: (1) Client reference callsâ€”we contact 2-3 clients mentioned by the candidate to confirm project duration and nature of work, (2) Invoice and payment verificationâ€”checking GST invoices, bank statement credits, or 26AS tax returns showing freelance income, (3) Portfolio reviewâ€”evaluating documented work products with timestamp verification, (4) Business registrationâ€”checking for proprietorship registrations or GST numbers active during the gap period. We understand the gig economy and validate genuine freelance work as legitimate career activity rather than treating it as an "unexplained gap."

Job search is a legitimate reason for employment gaps, especially in volatile markets or during recession periods. We verify this through: (1) Interview recordsâ€”checking for offer letters received and declined, or interview call records, (2) Skill enhancement documentationâ€”certifications or courses pursued during the job search, (3) Referencesâ€”contacting placement agencies or headhunters who assisted the candidate, (4) Financial recordsâ€”showing consistent living expenses indicating genuine job search rather than concurrent hidden employment, (5) LinkedIn activityâ€”reviewing job application activity and network posts from that period. Extended job searches (6+ months) in a strong economy may warrant additional scrutiny, but we assess contextually based on industry benchmarks.

Concealed employment is a serious red flag indicating potential termination for cause or legal issues. We detect this through: (1) EPFO/UAN record checks showing contributions during the "gap" period, (2) Form 16 or tax return analysis revealing salary income when candidate claimed unemployment, (3) Reference calls to companies not listed on the resume, (4) Social media activity showing employment at unlisted companies. If concealed employment is discovered, we categorize this as "Integrity Issue" rather than just "Unexplained Gap." The report includes evidence of the concealed employment and recommendation for HR review. This is particularly critical for BFSI sectors where concealment may indicate termination due to fraud or compliance violations.

Yes, caregiving responsibilities are increasingly recognized as legitimate career breaks. We verify family care gaps through: (1) Medical documentation for the family member being cared for (with privacy protections), (2) Address verification showing the candidate resided with the family member during that period, (3) Financial records showing support provided to the family, (4) Reference calls from family members or neighbors. We distinguish between "caregiving" and "unemployment" by looking for evidence of responsibility taking. Many progressive employers now view caregiving gaps positively as they demonstrate responsibility and emotional maturity. We flag any gaps where candidates falsely claim family care to hide termination, which is detectable through conflicting employment records.
