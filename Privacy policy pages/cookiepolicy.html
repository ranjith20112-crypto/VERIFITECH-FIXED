Understanding Cookies

What Are Cookies?

Small text files that make your web experience better

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit our website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to the website owners.

Identification

Cookies help us recognize your device and remember your preferences for future visits.

Analytics

They allow us to analyze how visitors interact with our site, helping us improve functionality.

Site Improvement

Cookies enable us to customize content and provide a personalized experience.

How Cookies Work

What Cookies Do We Use and Why?

Understanding the different types of cookies we employ to enhance your experience

Essential

Strictly Necessary

First-party cookies placed by Verifitech that are essential for the website to function properly. These cannot be disabled as they are required for basic site operations such as page navigation, security, and accessibility.

  • Session management
  • Security features

Analytics

Analytics & Performance

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. They assist us in improving site structure and content.

  • Google Analytics
  • Usage statistics
  • Performance metrics
Third Party

Third Party Cookies

Cookies placed by external parties such as YouTube, social media platforms, and advertising partners. These may track your browsing habits across different websites.

  • YouTube embeds
  • Social media
  • Advertising partners

Session vs. Persistent Cookies

Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser. They help maintain your session state during your visit.

Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a specified period or until manually deleted. They remember your preferences for future visits.
